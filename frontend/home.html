About The Algorithm

Learn how Genetic Algorithms work and why they are powerful tools for solving optimization problems. Explore the principles of natural selection, mutation, and evolution that drive the algorithm to find the best solutions.

A Genetic Algorithm (GA) is a search and optimization technique inspired by the process of natural selection in biology. It mimics evolution by generating a population of possible solutions and improving them step by step. The main goal is to find the most optimal solution for a given problem.

The algorithm works through key processes such as selection, crossover, and mutation. In each generation, the fittest solutions are chosen, combined, and slightly altered to create new candidates. Over time, this evolutionary cycle increases the chances of discovering high-quality solutions.

Genetic Algorithms are widely used in fields such as machine learning, engineering, and data science. They are especially powerful when dealing with complex or large search spaces where traditional methods struggle. By simulating evolution, GAs provide innovative and efficient ways to solve real-world challenges.
